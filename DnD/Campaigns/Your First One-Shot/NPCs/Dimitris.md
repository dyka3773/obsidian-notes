# Dimitris the Informant

A street-smart information broker who finds the party and leads them to the High Priest. He doesn't know the full truth, he's just trying to make coin and stay alive.

## Basic Info
- **Race/Type**: Human
- **Age**: 35
- **Role**: Information Broker / Merchant / Spy
- **Allegiance**: Neutral (self-serving)
- **Status**: Alive (quest-giver)

## Appearance
Dimitris is average-looking in a way that makes him forgettable, brown hair, brown eyes, nondescript clothing. This is intentional; he's built his career on not being noticed. He wears practical merchant's clothes and always carries a satchel full of "wares" (usually stolen goods or information). He fidgets constantly, tapping fingers, shifting weight, glancing around.

## Personality & Mannerisms
- **Quirks**:
  - Talks fast when nervous
  - Uses hand gestures excessively
  - Always has a deal or favor to propose
  - Defensive but not hostile
- **Speech Pattern**:
  - Casual tavern speech, lots of slang
  - "Listen, I got a proposition for you..."
  - "No questions, you understand? Just do the job."
  - Often repeats himself when anxious
- **Motivations**:
  - Easy money (the High Priest pays well)
  - Staying alive and out of trouble
  - Building connections and favors
- **Fears**:
  - Getting caught in the middle of something dangerous
  - The High Priest (who employs him indirectly)
  - Failing assignments and losing pay

## Backstory
Dimitris grew up on Thessaloniki's streets and learned early: information is currency. He built a network of contacts, merchants, guards, beggars, who feed him gossip and secrets. He sells what he hears to anyone willing to pay.

A few months ago, a mysterious man (one of [[High Priest Vasilis|Vasilis]]'s cultists) approached him with a lucrative offer: find capable adventurers and convince them to steal an artifact from the catacombs. Dimitris didn't ask questions, he was too well-paid. He assumed it was a simple heist job.

He still doesn't know about [[High Priest Vasilis|Vasilis]], the relic's true power, or Darius III.

## Relationship with Party
- **First Met**: In a tavern, approaching them with the "job offer"
- **Current Attitude**: Professional, friendly, but self-serving
- **The Pitch**: "I got a job for capable folks like you. Good pay, simple work. Steal artifact from a warlord, bring it to a very important man. Easy."
- **Payment**: 25 gp upfront, 75 gp on completion
- **Exit Strategy**: He plans to disappear once they complete the job

## Game Stats (NPC, not a combatant)
**AC**: 12 | **HP**: 4 | **Speed**: 30 ft.

He's not built for combat and will flee if violence starts. Perfectly ordinary human commoner/merchant.

## For the Table
- **Information Source**: He can answer basic questions about the High Priest (publicly known info only)
- **Guidance**: He'll lead the party to the temple district to meet [[High Priest Vasilis|Vasilis]]
- **Red Herring**: Players might suspect he's the villain; he's not (he's just caught up in it)
- **Development**: After they learn the truth, the party might want to question him about his employer

## If Party Interrogates Him Later
- He genuinely doesn't know much: "I was hired by some guy in a hood! I never asked questions!"
- He can describe his contact (one of [[High Priest Vasilis|Vasilis]]'s cultists)
- He's terrified of both the party and the High Priest
- He'll provide any information to stay alive
- Possible redemption: Help them track down other cultists in exchange for protection

## DM Notes
- Dimitris is comic relief and plot device, use him to set the adventure in motion
- Don't make him annoying; he's nervous and practical
- If the party likes him, he could become a recurring contact
- He's useful for delivering messages/information later
- His fear of the High Priest makes him easy to manipulate once the truth is revealed


## Make the offer Irresistible

### For Greedy Characters:
Dimitri offers 50 gp upfront, 100 gp on completion (that's a LOT for level 1!)
"You could buy weapons, armor, a horse, lodging for weeks..."

### For Heroic Characters:
Dimitri adds: "The High Priest helps the poor and sick. He needs this to keep the city safe."
"Without the relic secured, dangerous forces might claim it."

"You might be the first to see something lost for 2000 years."
