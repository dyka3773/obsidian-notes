**From**: [[Campaign - The Relic of Alexander|The Relic of Alexander]] one-shot
**Location**: [[Thessaloniki (Hub City)]], Harbor District (The Salty Trident tavern)

## Appearance

**Physical Description**:
- Average height, wiry build
- Sharp, intelligent eyes (constantly scanning for danger or opportunity)
- Well-groomed beard, practical clothing (dark tunic, leather vest)
- Always has a hidden dagger or two
- Looks like a merchant, acts like a rogue

**Mannerisms**:
- Speaks in low tones (never raises his voice in public)
- Gestures minimally (trained not to draw attention)
- Always sits with his back to the wall, facing the door
- Drinks slowly (stays sharp)

**Voice**: Smooth, measured, occasionally sarcastic

---
## Personality

**Core Traits**:
- **Self-Preservation First**: Won't risk his life unless the pay is extraordinary
- **Reliable (Within Limits)**: Keeps his word on deals. Reputation is currency
- **Amoral, Not Evil**: Doesn't care about good vs. evil, just business
- **Clever, Not Brave**: Uses information and connections, not violence

**Motivations**:
- **Make money** (primary)
- **Stay alive** (always)
- **Build influence** (long-term goal: become indispensable to multiple factions)

**Fears**:
- Being double-crossed
- Faction wars catching him in the crossfire
- Losing his network (his only real power)

**Quirks**:
- Always knows someone who knows someone
- Refuses to give information for free (even to friends)
- Has an escape route from every location he frequents

## Background

### Before the One-Shot
- Grew up in Thessaloniki's slums
- Learned early: information is more valuable than muscle
- Built a network of contacts (sailors, smugglers, low-level cultists, corrupt guards)
- Operates in moral gray zones (not a villain, not a hero)
### During the One-Shot
- Hired the party to infiltrate the catacombs (on behalf of [[High Priest Vasilis]])
- Likely didn't know [[High Priest Vasilis|Vasilis]]'s true intentions (or didn't care)
- Paid the party as promised (established credibility)
### Post-One-Shot Status
**Key Question**: Was [[DnD/Campaigns/Your First One-Shot/NPCs/Dimitris|Dimitris]] complicit in [[High Priest Vasilis|Vasilis]]'s plan?

**Answer**: **No** (recommended interpretation for campaign use)
- He was a middleman (hired to find capable people)
- He didn't ask questions (plausible deniability)
- When the truth came out, he likely felt *relieved* ([[High Priest Vasilis|Vasilis]]'s failure doesn't implicate him)

**This makes him a useful recurring NPC** (not an enemy, but not entirely trustworthy).

## Role in the Campaign

### As a Recurring Contact

**What [[DnD/Campaigns/Your First One-Shot/NPCs/Dimitris|Dimitris]] Offers**:
1. **Information** (rumors, faction movements, relic locations)
2. **Jobs** (morally gray work, smuggling, retrieval, espionage)
3. **Introductions** (can broker meetings with faction representatives)
4. **Black Market Access** (stolen goods, contraband, forged documents)
5. **Escape Routes** (if the party needs to disappear from Thessaloniki)

**What [[DnD/Campaigns/Your First One-Shot/NPCs/Dimitris|Dimitris]] Wants**:
1. **Gold** (always)
2. **Information** (the party's adventures are valuable gossip)
3. **Favors** (occasionally asks the party to do something for him)
4. **Survival** (won't work with the party if it endangers him)

## DM Notes: Using [[DnD/Campaigns/Your First One-Shot/NPCs/Dimitris|Dimitris]]

### When to Use Him
- **Between adventures** (he approaches the party with a new job)
- **When the party needs information** (they seek him out)
- **To introduce faction representatives** (he brokers meetings)
- **As a moral compass check** (his pragmatism highlights the party's choices)
### When NOT to Overuse Him
- Don't make him the party's only contact (diversify NPCs)
- Don't force him into every session (he's a tool, not a main character)
- Don't make him betray the party *unless* they give him a reason (he's reliable within his limits)
### Evolving the Relationship
**If the party treats him well**:
- He offers better deals (discounts, exclusive information)
- He warns them about dangers (even if it's not profitable)
- He might call in a favor (but it's reasonable)
**If the party treats him poorly**:
- He raises prices (trust tax)
- He withholds critical information
- He eventually cuts them off
**If the party becomes powerful**:
- [[DnD/Campaigns/Your First One-Shot/NPCs/Dimitris|Dimitris]] wants to stay on their good side (they're influential now)
- He might ask them to protect him from a faction
- He could become a loyal ally (rare, but possible)
