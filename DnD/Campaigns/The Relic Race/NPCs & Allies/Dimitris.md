# Dimitris - Information Broker

**Role**: Recurring contact, information broker, quest-giver
**From**: [[Campaign - The Relic of Alexander|The Relic of Alexander]] one-shot
**Location**: [[Thessaloniki (Hub City)]], Harbor District (The Salty Trident tavern)

---

## Quick Reference

**Race**: Human (Greek)
**Age**: Mid-30s
**Occupation**: Information broker, smuggler, "facilitator"
**Alignment**: True Neutral (self-interested, but reliable)
**Disposition to Party**: Neutral ‚Üí Friendly (if paid well and treated fairly)

---
## Appearance

**Physical Description**:
- Average height, wiry build
- Sharp, intelligent eyes (constantly scanning for danger or opportunity)
- Well-groomed beard, practical clothing (dark tunic, leather vest)
- Always has a hidden dagger or two
- Looks like a merchant, acts like a rogue

**Mannerisms**:
- Speaks in low tones (never raises his voice in public)
- Gestures minimally (trained not to draw attention)
- Always sits with his back to the wall, facing the door
- Drinks slowly (stays sharp)

**Voice**: Smooth, measured, occasionally sarcastic

---
## Personality

**Core Traits**:
- **Self-Preservation First**: Won't risk his life unless the pay is extraordinary
- **Reliable (Within Limits)**: Keeps his word on deals. Reputation is currency
- **Amoral, Not Evil**: Doesn't care about good vs. evil, just business
- **Clever, Not Brave**: Uses information and connections, not violence

**Motivations**:
- **Make money** (primary)
- **Stay alive** (always)
- **Build influence** (long-term goal: become indispensable to multiple factions)

**Fears**:
- Being double-crossed
- Faction wars catching him in the crossfire
- Losing his network (his only real power)

**Quirks**:
- Always knows someone who knows someone
- Refuses to give information for free (even to friends)
- Has an escape route from every location he frequents

---
## Background

### Before the One-Shot
- Grew up in Thessaloniki's slums
- Learned early: information is more valuable than muscle
- Built a network of contacts (sailors, smugglers, low-level cultists, corrupt guards)
- Operates in moral gray zones (not a villain, not a hero)
### During the One-Shot
- Hired the party to infiltrate the catacombs (on behalf of [[High Priest Vasilis]])
- Likely didn't know Vasilis's true intentions (or didn't care)
- Paid the party as promised (established credibility)
### Post-One-Shot Status
**Key Question**: Was Dimitris complicit in Vasilis's plan?

**Answer**: **No** (recommended interpretation for campaign use)
- He was a middleman (hired to find capable people)
- He didn't ask questions (plausible deniability)
- When the truth came out, he likely felt *relieved* (Vasilis's failure doesn't implicate him)

**This makes him a useful recurring NPC** (not an enemy, but not entirely trustworthy).

## Role in the Campaign

### As a Recurring Contact

**What Dimitris Offers**:
1. **Information** (rumors, faction movements, relic locations)
2. **Jobs** (morally gray work‚Äîsmuggling, retrieval, espionage)
3. **Introductions** (can broker meetings with faction representatives)
4. **Black Market Access** (stolen goods, contraband, forged documents)
5. **Escape Routes** (if the party needs to disappear from Thessaloniki)

**What Dimitris Wants**:
1. **Gold** (always)
2. **Information** (the party's adventures are valuable gossip)
3. **Favors** (occasionally asks the party to do something for him)
4. **Survival** (won't work with the party if it endangers him)

---
### Quest Hooks from Dimitris

#### 1. "The Cult's Remnants"
**Hook**: Dimitris has information on where surviving cultists are hiding.
**Price**: 200 gp or a favor (retrieve something he smuggled and lost)
**Adventure**: Track down Cult safe houses in the slums

---

#### 2. "The Faction Spy"
**Hook**: Dimitris suspects a faction spy is operating in Thessaloniki. He'll pay the party to identify them.
**Price**: 300 gp + information about a relic
**Adventure**: Investigate, gather clues, confront the spy (combat or social)

---

#### 3. "Smuggling Job"
**Hook**: Dimitris needs to move contraband past the city guard. He'll pay the party to escort the shipment (or provide a distraction).
**Price**: 150 gp + future favor
**Complication**: The cargo is stolen goods, a relic component, or something dangerous

---

#### 4. "The Pirate's Message"
**Hook**: A pirate captain wants to meet with the party. Dimitris brokers the introduction‚Äîfor a fee.
**Price**: 100 gp
**Adventure**: Meet the pirate captain, negotiate, or get ambushed

---

#### 5. "Vasilis's Legacy"
**Hook**: Dimitris heard a rumor that Vasilis left behind hidden notes or research. He'll split the reward if the party retrieves it.
**Price**: 50/50 split of whatever they find
**Adventure**: Search Vasilis's old haunts (Church, safe houses, archives)

---

#### 6. "The Auction"
**Hook**: A relic (or alleged relic) is being auctioned in a pirate haven. Dimitris can get the party an invitation.
**Price**: 10% of whatever the party sells the relic for (or 500 gp upfront)
**Adventure**: Travel to pirate haven, outbid or steal the relic

## Relationships

### With the Party
- **Initial**: Neutral (transactional)
- **If treated well**: Friendly (offers better deals, warns them of danger)
- **If betrayed**: Hostile (cuts them off from his network, might sell information *about* them)

### With Factions
- **[[Northern Power (Macedonian Bloc)]]**: Has low-level contacts, but not aligned
- **[[Maritime City-State]]**: Works with smugglers tied to them
- **[[Cult of Vasilis (Remnants)]]**: Knows where some members hide (but won't help them)
- **[[Scholarly Order]]**: Occasionally sells information to scholars

**Key Point**: Dimitris doesn't take sides‚Äîhe sells to everyone.

## DM Notes: Using Dimitris

### When to Use Him
- **Between adventures** (he approaches the party with a new job)
- **When the party needs information** (they seek him out)
- **To introduce faction representatives** (he brokers meetings)
- **As a moral compass check** (his pragmatism highlights the party's choices)

### When NOT to Overuse Him
- Don't make him the party's only contact (diversify NPCs)
- Don't force him into every session (he's a tool, not a main character)
- Don't make him betray the party *unless* they give him a reason (he's reliable within his limits)

### Evolving the Relationship
**If the party treats him well**:
- He offers better deals (discounts, exclusive information)
- He warns them about dangers (even if it's not profitable)
- He might call in a favor (but it's reasonable)

**If the party treats him poorly**:
- He raises prices (trust tax)
- He withholds critical information
- He eventually cuts them off

**If the party becomes powerful**:
- Dimitris wants to stay on their good side (they're influential now)
- He might ask them to protect him from a faction
- He could become a loyal ally (rare, but possible)

## Adventure Hooks: Dimitris in Danger

### 1. "The Hit"
A faction puts a price on Dimitris's head (he knows too much). He begs the party to protect him.

### 2. "The Betrayal"
Dimitris is arrested (for smuggling or espionage). The party must decide: rescue him, let him rot, or use him as leverage?

### 3. "The Rival"
A rival information broker moves into Thessaloniki. Dimitris hires the party to "convince" them to leave.

### 4. "The Debt"
Dimitris owes money to dangerous people (pirates or a crime lord). He needs the party's help to pay or fight his way out.
