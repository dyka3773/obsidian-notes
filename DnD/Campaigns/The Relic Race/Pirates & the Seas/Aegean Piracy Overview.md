# Aegean Piracy Overview

Pirates, privateers, and naval power in the Aegean Sea.

---

## Overview

**Core Concept**: The Aegean Sea is **pirate-infested** but not lawless‚Äîthere are independent raiders *and* an organized alliance (like the Brethren Court from Pirates of the Caribbean).

**Why Pirates Matter to the Campaign**:
- Relics are transported by sea ‚Üí Pirates intercept them
- [[Maritime City-State]] hires pirates as privateers
- Party might need to negotiate, fight, or join them
- Naval adventures add variety to the campaign

**Tone**: **Pirates of the Caribbean meets Mediterranean history**
- Swashbuckling action
- Moral ambiguity (not all pirates are evil)
- Treasure, sea battles, and legendary ships

---

## The Two Types of Pirates

### 1. Independent Pirates (Chaotic)

**Who They Are**:
- Small crews (10-30 people per ship)
- Opportunistic raiders
- No allegiance to any faction or alliance
- Motivated by: Survival, greed, or revenge

**What They Do**:
- Raid merchant ships
- Smuggle goods between city-states
- Occasionally work as mercenaries (if the pay is good)

**Threat Level**: Low-Medium (dangerous to lone travelers, but not organized)

**Examples**:
- A desperate captain trying to feed their crew
- A former naval officer turned outlaw
- Refugees-turned-raiders (driven to piracy by poverty)

**Campaign Use**:
- Minor encounters (party fights them off or negotiates)
- Potential allies if the party offers better payment than victims would
- Source of rumors (pirates hear everything)

---

### 2. The Pirate Alliance (Organized)

**Name**: *The Aegean Compact* (or use a different name if you prefer)

**Inspired By**: Brethren Court (Pirates of the Caribbean)

**Who They Are**:
- A coalition of the most powerful pirate captains
- Each captain controls a fleet (3-10 ships)
- Loosely organized (they meet, vote, make decisions‚Äîbut enforce them selectively)

**Structure**:
- **The Pirate Lords** (5-7 captains with seats at the table)
- **The Code** (rules pirates agree to follow‚Äîmore guidelines than laws)
- **Safe Havens** (islands where the Alliance guarantees safety)

**What They Do**:
- Control key trade routes
- Protect their members from navies
- Negotiate with city-states (sometimes as privateers, sometimes as threats)
- Hunt down pirates who break the Code

**Threat Level**: High (a unified pirate armada is a naval superpower)

**Campaign Use**:
- The party can join the Alliance (become pirates!)
- Negotiate with Pirate Lords (for ships, information, or passage)
- Fight the Alliance (if they're aligned with [[Maritime City-State]])

### The Pirate Code (Rules of the Alliance)

1. **Parley**: Any captain (or crew) can demand negotiation before combat (must be honored)
2. **Fair Share**: Treasure is divided among the crew (cheating = mutiny)
3. **Right of Challenge**: Anyone can challenge a captain for command (trial by combat or vote)
4. **Respect the Havens**: No violence in Alliance safe harbors (penalty = exile or death)
5. **No Betrayal of the Alliance**: Snitching to navies = hunted by all pirates
6. **Honor the Vote**: When Pirate Lords vote, the majority rules (even if you disagree)

**"The Code is more what you'd call guidelines than actual rules."** ‚ÄìJack Sparrow

(Enforcement depends on who you are and who you've angered.)

## Pirate Safe Havens (Islands)

### 1. **Tortuga** (or rename to something Greek, e.g., "Skiathos Haven")

**Description**: Lawless pirate port, taverns, black markets, ship repairs

**Who's There**:
- Pirates of all kinds
- Smugglers, fences, mercenaries
- A few Alliance representatives

**What the Party Can Do**:
- Hire a ship and crew
- Buy/sell contraband
- Gather rumors about relics
- Get into bar fights (inevitably)

**Danger**: No laws = no protection (watch your back)

---

### 2. **The Shipwreck Graveyard** (Hidden Island)

**Description**: Cove filled with wrecked ships (some ancient, some recent), used as a secret meeting place for Pirate Lords

**Who's There**:
- Pirate Lords during major votes
- Scavengers looking for salvage
- Rumored to be haunted

**What the Party Can Do**:
- Attend a Pirate Lord council (if invited)
- Search wrecks for treasure (dangerous‚Äîtraps, ghosts, curses)
- Witness pirate politics firsthand

**Danger**: Trespassing without invitation = death sentence from the Alliance

---

## Naval Mechanics (Using Unearthed Arcana)

### Ship Combat Basics

**Reference**: *Unearthed Arcana: Of Ships and the Sea* (Wizards of the Coast)

**Key Rules**:
- Ships have AC, HP, speed
- Crew actions: Sail, attack (cannons/ballistae), board, repair
- Officers (captain, helmsman, gunner) give bonuses

**Simplified for Play**:
- **Chase scenes**: Contested checks (Helmsman's skill vs. enemy's)
- **Broadsides**: Cannons deal massive damage to ships but are slow to reload
- **Boarding**: When ships are adjacent, melee combat begins

**DM Tip**: Don't bog down every encounter with full ship rules‚Äîuse them for major battles, simplify for minor ones.

---

### Party Ship Options

**If the party gets a ship** (from [[Maritime City-State]], Pirate Alliance, or purchase):

1. **Sloop** (Small, fast, 20-30 crew)
   - Good for: Exploration, smuggling, quick escapes
   - Cost: 10,000 gp

2. **Brigantine** (Medium, balanced, 50-70 crew)
   - Good for: Combat, trading, piracy
   - Cost: 25,000 gp

3. **Galleon** (Large, powerful, 100+ crew)
   - Good for: Naval battles, transporting large groups
   - Cost: 50,000+ gp

**Crew Management**:
- Hire crew in ports (sailors, gunners, marines)
- Pay wages (10-50 gp per crew member per month)
- Morale matters (low morale = mutiny risk)

## Pirates & the Relic Race

### Why Pirates Care About Relics

1. **Treasure value** (relics sell for fortunes)
2. **Leverage** (sell to highest bidder‚Äîfactions will pay)
3. **Supernatural power** (some pirates want magic for themselves)
4. **Legends** (pirates love becoming legendary)

### How Pirates Interfere

**Scenario 1: Pirate Ambush**
The party is transporting a relic by sea ‚Üí Pirates attack to steal it.

**Scenario 2: Bidding War**
Pirates captured a relic and auction it to factions (party can outbid or steal it back).

**Scenario 3: Alliance Offer**
Pirate Lords offer the party a ship and crew in exchange for a cut of relic profits.

**Scenario 4: Cursed Treasure**
Pirates stole a relic, got cursed, and beg the party to lift it.

## Adventure Hooks: Pirate Edition

### 1. "The Captain's Bargain"
A charismatic pirate captain (Jack Sparrow type) offers the party a map to a relic‚Äîif they help him settle a score with a rival.

### 2. "Mutiny!"
The party hires a ship, but the crew mutinies mid-voyage. Do they fight, negotiate, or join the mutiny?

### 3. "The Ghost Ship"
Sailors report seeing *The Ghost Wind* (cursed pirate ship). Investigate or avoid?

### 4. "Pirate Parley"
The party is invited to a Pirate Lord council. They must navigate politics, duels, and intrigue‚Äîwithout a ship to back them up.

### 5. "The Black Market Auction"
A relic appears on the black market in a pirate haven. Factions, pirates, and the party all compete to buy (or steal) it.

### 6. "The Code"
The party accidentally breaks the Pirate Code. Now they're hunted by the Alliance‚Äîcan they apologize, fight, or flee?

## Pirate Stat Blocks (Quick Reference)

### Pirate (Common Crew)
Use: **Bandit** (Monster Manual p. 343)
- AC 12, HP 11
- Scimitar +3 to hit, 1d6+1 damage
- Light crossbow +3 to hit, 1d8+1 damage

### Pirate Captain (Named NPC)
Use: **Bandit Captain** (MM p. 344) with modifications
- AC 15, HP 65
- Multiattack (2 attacks)
- Scimitar +5 to hit, 1d6+3 damage
- Special: Parley (Charisma-based negotiation advantage)

### Pirate Lord (Major NPC)
Use: **Veteran** (MM p. 350) or **Gladiator** (MM p. 346)
- AC 17, HP 58-112
- Multiattack (2-3 attacks)
- Legendary pirate captains might have unique abilities (e.g., Jack Sparrow has Uncanny Dodge, Barbossa has Strategic Mind)

### Ghost Ship Crew (Cursed Pirates)
Use: **Specter** (MM p. 279) or **Wight** (MM p. 300)
- Incorporeal, necrotic attacks
- Vulnerable to radiant damage

## Campaign Integration

### Pirate Faction Tracker

| Pirate Group | Disposition to Party | Resources | Goals |
|--------------|---------------------|-----------|-------|
| **Independent Pirates** | Neutral (opportunistic) | Small ships, desperate | Survive, profit |
| **Aegean Compact (Alliance)** | Neutral (test the party first) | Large fleet, safe havens | Control seas, profit from relics |
| **[[Maritime City-State]]'s Privateers** | Depends on party's alignment | State funding, legal protection | Secure relics for the state |

### When to Use Pirates

**Early Campaign** (Levels 2-4):
- Minor pirate encounters (build to naval combat)
- Introduce Pirate Alliance through rumors

**Mid Campaign** (Levels 5-8):
- Party gets a ship or joins Alliance
- Major naval battles, pirate politics

**Late Campaign** (Levels 9+):
- Pirate Lords become key allies or enemies
- Epic sea battles involving multiple factions

## DM Notes: Running Pirate Adventures

### Tone Balance
- **Serious moments**: Naval combat, betrayals, cursed ships
- **Fun moments**: Bar fights, treasure hunts, Jack Sparrow-style absurdity

### Pirates Aren't Always Villains
- Some are refugees, some are rebels, some are just trying to survive
- Let the party decide how to interact (fight, negotiate, join)

### Use the Environment
- **Storms** (Nature checks to survive)
- **Sea monsters** (krakens, sirens, etc.)
- **Island exploration** (uncharted lands, ancient ruins)
